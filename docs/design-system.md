# Design System

## Brand Voice

Precise, cinematic, restrained, source-aware, and useful to a working creative team. Avoid inflated claims and game-lobby language.

## Layout Principles

- One primary workspace per view.
- Dense information with deliberate whitespace and thin rules.
- Stable navigation, search, filters, and evidence access.
- No nested decorative cards or floating marketing sections.
- Mobile collapses to one column without trapping horizontal or vertical scroll.

## Color Tokens

- Background: `#0e0f10`
- Panel: `#17191b`
- Line: `#34373a`
- Text: `#e2dfd8`
- Muted: `#96999d`
- Accent: `#c6a967`
- Positive evidence: `#9abb9f`
- Inferred evidence: `#9bb0ca`
- Warning/unresolved: `#d0a46c`

## Typography

Use a readable serif for project titles and section headings, paired with the operating system sans-serif for controls and dense metadata. Letter spacing stays neutral except for small folio labels.

## Card Components

Cards represent repeated entities only. Character cards combine manifest-bound art, faction, full name, role, narrative function, first appearance, relationship count, importance, and evidence status.

## Status Badges

Statuses use restrained outlines and text. Red is reserved for actual errors. Provider absence and unresolved evidence use neutral amber, not alarm styling.

## Evidence Tags

`explicit`, `inferred`, `ambiguous`, `contradictory`, and `unresolved` remain visible and clickable. Evidence opens in a side drawer with quote, source, chunk, and confidence.

## Actor Mode UI

A stable character selector sits beside a focused preparation file. The file separates objective, emotional arc, subtext, playable actions, relationship beats, costume/props, and voice/physicality.

## Visual Bible UI

Visual rules are data, not decoration: palette, materials, composition, camera language, costume/props, and prohibited styles are displayed as a production reference.

## Theme Directions

### A. Manuscript Theme, planned

Historical and literary work: paper texture, ink rules, annotations, archive numbers, handwritten marks, and desaturated brown-gold tokens.

### B. Cinematic Bible Theme, implemented

Film and television work: deep gray surfaces, still-frame cards, shot numbering, slate details, subtitle-like hierarchy, and brass accents.

### C. Game Codex Theme, planned

RPG and worldbuilding work: faction marks, character tiers, skill panels, map nodes, quest lines, and equipment indexes without glossy game-lobby styling.
